"There's no point counting the stairs before you start climbing."

I'm drawn to this quote because it reflects the kind of path I've chosen for myself. The career I'm pursuing doesn't come with a clear, followable structure or a well defined roadmap. That uncertainty can feel overwhelming, especially when there's so much that is still unknown and so much left to figure out. But instead of getting stuck in that, I've learned to approach things step by step. I focus on understanding what's in front of me, figuring out how things actually work, rather than panicking about the scale of what I don't yet know.

That's also why carbon removal feels like the right space for me. It carries a larger vision of creating real change, something that directly contributes to the public good and addresses a need that is urgent right now. Being part of something that is still being shaped, while also having the potential to create meaningful impact, is what keeps me genuinely interested and invested.

Alongside this, I'm drawn to strong branding and storytelling, especially the kind that builds a clear sense of purpose and brings people together around ideas that matter. I'm equally interested in going beyond the surface level, understanding how a business truly runs, how decisions are made, how systems function, and how everything comes together behind the scenes. I naturally lean towards learning the core ins and outs rather than just observing outcomes.

I'm constantly trying to learn and improve in a way that feels intentional. On one side, I actively explore and work with AI tools, understanding how they can be used effectively. On the other, I consciously take time away from fast moving screens to reduce constant stimulation, improve focus, and build a healthier attention span. For me, it's about creating a balance that allows me to think clearly, stay grounded, and keep moving forward with purpose.
